Spinal Decompression Therapy

Do you suffer from degenerative disc disease, herniated or bulging disc, sciatica or other spinal problems? Has your physician suggested that you consider surgery? You may want to consider Spinal Decompression Therapy. Spinal Decompression Therapy has been proven effective in treating degenerative discs, facet syndrome, sciatica, herniated discs and spinal stenosis. If you already had back surgery,Spinal Decompression Therapy can still be considered (under certain conditions) if you suffer from failed back surgery syndrome. Non-Surgical Spinal Decompression Therapy is a revolutionary new technology used primarily to treat disc injuries in the neck and low back.This treatment option is very safe and utilizes Health Canada cleared equipment to apply distraction forces to spinal structures in a precise and graduated manner. Normally when pulls are exerted on the spine such as conventional traction the muscles respond by tightening. In Spinal Decompression Therapy spinal distraction is offset by cycles of partial relaxation.The body does not respond by reflexively tightening the muscles thus making this technique extremely comfortable and effective.

What does Non-Sugical Spinal Decompression do?

Firstly, it stretches the overlying soft tissues(muscles, ligaments, tendons and connective tissues) of the spinal area being treated. Spinal Decompression has shown the ability to gently separate the vertebrae from each other specifically their facet joints to afford you more range of motion. This gentle separation of the vertebrae also creates a vacuum inside the discs that we are targeting. This vacuum effect is known as negative intra-discal pressure. The negative pressure may induce the retraction of the herniated or bulging disc into the inside of the disc and off the nerve root, thecal sac or both. By doing so this removes pressure off the nerve root,thecal sac or both. This event occurs microscopically with each visit but cumulatively over four to six weeks of treatments.The results are quite dramatic. Spinal discs do not get oxygen and fresh blood on a consistent basis.This may lead to disc dessication.Decompression and motion can enhance healing by promoting the diffusion of water, oxygen and nutrient rich fluids from the outside of the disc to the inside.The nutrients enable the torn and degenerative disc fibres to heal.Treatments run about twenty (20) minutes in length. Many patients enjoy the treatment as it is usually quite comfortable and well tolerated.
