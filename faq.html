Frequently Asked Questions

Question: Am I doing harm to myself by "self-adjusting" my spine?
Over the years I have encountered a number of patients who try to "self adjust" themselves by either contorting or stretching their body to achieve the "cracking" or the cavitation. In essence they are trying to duplicate what a Chiropractor does and in most cases the patient is not successful. This failure by the patient to obtain the release may lead to microtrauma to the area and subsequent repeat attempts may cause chronic abuse to the surrounding joint structures. This self-treatment is highly non recommended. Chiropractic care is a safe,non-intrusive procedure that will not hurt you as long as it is provided by a qualified Chiropractor.
Question: What is the difference between an inversion table and spinal decompression therapy?
On an inversion table you are hanging by your ankles and this can be rather painful. You are using your body weight and gravity as an assist to generate the pull.Your time stretching may be severely limited on an inversion table due to increased blood flow to your head and ensuing lightheadedness. On an inversion table you are limited to a static pull. With spinal decompression therapy you are either lying supine or prone on a treatment table, not inverted. Treatment times are 20 min or more and the amount of pull can be varied.There is no pain to be experienced as some patients even fall asleep! Spinal decompression therapy is computerized and not manual with either a static or dynamic pull applied. This cannot be be duplicated with an inversion table. Both the neck and low back are treated with spinal decompression therapy.
